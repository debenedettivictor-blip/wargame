## Most Common War Scenarios

### Scenario 1: "Standard Entente Victory" (18/30 games, 60%)
Germany invades Belgium or France directly. Entente coordination + Russian numbers overwhelm Central Powers by Turn 5-6. France takes Alsace. Russia dominates East. Austria collapses from desertion.

### Scenario 2: "Austrian Peace Victory" (5/30 games, 17%)
Austria accumulates +3 VP/turn, avoids war entirely or enters very late. Never-at-war bonus of +3. Ends with 15-21 VP from pure peace dividend.

### Scenario 3: "Ottoman Wildcard" (4/30 games, 13%)
Ottoman uses neutrality to profit, then either conquers Caucasus on Central side or backstabs Austria on Entente side. Minor nation recruitment (Bulgaria, Romania) creates Balkan advantages.

### Scenario 4: "German Blitz" (2/30 games, 7%)
Germany gets lucky dice on Schlieffen Plan, knocks France out early. Requires exceptional rolls (6 vs 1 type margins). Even then, Eastern front remains contested.

### Scenario 5: "Arms Dealer Victory" (1/30 games, 3%)
Krupp plays both sides, loans to everyone, manufactures war, profits from dividends and sales. Only works when Krupp aggressively sells to BOTH sides.

## Key Strategic Patterns Observed

### 1. Austria's Peace VP is Overpowered
Austria's +3 VP/turn is the single strongest mechanic in the game. Over 6 turns of peace, Austria accumulates 18 VP + 3 never-war bonus = 21 VP, which beats nearly every war outcome. Austria won 43% of all games despite having the weakest military position. The rational strategy for Austria is ALWAYS to avoid war as long as possible.

### 2. France Has a Structural Disadvantage
With 0 peace VP/turn and 0 peace gold/turn, France MUST go to war to score any VP. But France can only realistically gain ~10 VP from war (Alsace 5 + battles 5). Meanwhile, Austria scores 18+ VP by doing nothing. France's only path to victory requires crushing military success AND early war.

### 3. The Entente Almost Always Wins Militarily
In 28 war games, the Entente won the Western front 93% of the time. The combination of France + Britain + fortress Paris is nearly unbeatable. Russia's large army dominates the East in most scenarios. The Central Powers can only win with exceptional dice or if Britain stays out.

### 4. Germany Cannot Win a Two-Front War
Germany's strong economy is consumed by war costs and blockade. The Schlieffen Plan fails ~90% of the time against a prepared France. Germany's best games (13, 19 VP) required multiple lucky dice rolls. The two-front war is structurally unwinnable for Germany without dramatic luck.

### 5. Austria's Desertion Mechanic is Devastating
Losing 1-2 divisions per turn while at war means Austria goes from 8-10 Army to 0-2 within 3-4 turns. Combined with stability collapse, Austria is essentially a ticking bomb once war starts. This makes Austria's peace strategy even more rational.

### 6. Britain Is a Kingmaker, Not a Winner
Britain's naval blockade decides wars but generates minimal VP for Britain itself. Britain averaged only 4.5 VP -- the lowest of any major power. The blockade mechanic needs a VP reward for Britain.

### 7. Arms Dealers Are Trapped
The -8 VP no-war penalty forces dealers to provoke war, but war doesn't guarantee dealer victory. Krupp won once; Schneider never won. The dealer role is high-effort, low-reward. They exist mainly as a narrative engine to force conflict.

### 8. Ottoman Benefits Most from Flexibility
Ottoman's lack of starting alliances is actually an advantage. Ottoman can join either side, time entry perfectly, and focus on the Caucasus (which is consistently winnable). Ottoman's average VP (10.0) is third-best.

## Balance Improvement Suggestions

### Critical Issues

**1. Reduce Austria's Peace VP from +3 to +1 per turn**
Austria's peace VP makes them the overwhelming favorite. At +3/turn, Austria has no incentive to participate in the game's core mechanic (war). Reducing to +1/turn would make peace Austria ~9 VP (6 peace + 3 never-war), competitive but not dominant. Austria should need to take SOME risk to win.

**2. Give France +1 Peace VP/turn and +1 Peace Gold/turn**
France's complete lack of peacetime scoring makes them the most disadvantaged major power. Even +1 VP/turn would give France a 6 VP floor, making pure-war strategies less desperate. The gold income would help France build up for war.

**3. Add VP for British Blockade: +1 VP per turn of active blockade**
Britain's kingmaker role is narratively interesting but mechanically unrewarding. The blockade is the most powerful economic weapon in the game but generates 0 VP. Even +1 VP/turn would give Britain 2-3 extra VP in a typical game.

**4. Reduce Austria's Desertion to -1 Division/Turn (not 1-2)**
The desertion mechanic makes Austria's military worthless within 2-3 turns, which FURTHER incentivizes the peace-only strategy. Reducing to a flat -1/turn would make Austria viable as a military power while still reflecting internal tensions.

### Moderate Issues

**5. Increase Arms Dealer No-War Penalty to -12 VP (from -8)**
This sounds counterintuitive, but the current -8 penalty isn't severe enough relative to dealer VP ceilings. Dealers can still finish at 0 VP in peace games with some sales. Making the penalty -12 would make dealer peace outcomes truly catastrophic, driving more aggressive crisis manufacturing.

Alternatively: give dealers +1 VP per turn of peace to offset the penalty slightly, but keep the -8 as a "no war at all" penalty.

**6. Give Germany a "Fortress East Prussia" Bonus (+2 defense in East)**
Germany historically had strong Eastern fortifications. This would help Germany survive the Eastern front and make the two-front war less automatically losing. It also creates more interesting strategic choices for Russia.

**7. Ottoman Should Start with 1 Alliance (Germany or Britain)**
Starting with zero alliances makes Ottoman's diplomatic path interesting but somewhat arbitrary. A starting alliance would ground Ottoman's strategic choices.

**8. Schneider Needs a Buff**
Schneider has less gold, less industry, and sells to the Entente (which is already militarily favored). Consider giving Schneider +1 VP per Entente victory or +2g starting gold.

### Minor Tweaks

**9. Belgium Should Give +3 VP (not +2)** -- Makes the Belgium gambit more tempting vs. the British entry risk.

**10. Russia's Slow Mobilization Could Be 2.5 Turns** (round down = 2 turns if started early) -- Russia's 3-turn mobilization means they're often not ready for Turn 4 wars. A slight buff would make Russian early mobilization more viable.
