## Design Insights

### Faction VP vs Personal VP Tension

The game's genius is that **faction VP and personal VP often conflict**:

| Conflict | Faction Impact | Personal Impact |
|----------|----------------|-----------------|
| Chancellor overrules Kaiser | Bad for Germany (internal chaos) | +4 VP for Chancellor |
| General launches Plan XVII | Risky for France | +3/+6 VP for General |
| Duma Rep triggers revolution | Destroys Russia | +8 VP for Duma Rep |
| Young Turk coups Sultan | Destabilizes Ottoman | +3 VP for Young Turk |
| Conrad forces early war | Possibly fatal for Austria | +4 VP for Conrad |
| Krupp sells to enemy | Hurts Germany | +2 VP per secret sale |

### The "Teammate Problem"
- **Russia** has the most internal conflict: Tsar vs War Minister vs literal traitor (Duma Rep)
- **Germany** has institutional friction: Kaiser's ego vs Chancellor's diplomacy vs Chief's militarism
- **Austria** is the simplest: Emperor vs Conrad, but the stakes are existential
- **Ottoman** is cleanest: Sultan vs Young Turk, winner takes all
- **Britain** is most cooperative: PM/First Lord/Foreign Sec have compatible goals (unusual!)
- **Arms Dealers** have the best design: internal competition (Krupp vs Vickers for control) PLUS external scheming

| Aggressive, wants to fight | French General or Conrad |

| New to games, wants simple role | German Chief of Staff |
