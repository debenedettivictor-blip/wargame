The Guns of August

A Grand Strategy Game
Europe, 1910 — The Powder Keg Awaits a Spark
⚔ The Premise

The year is 1910. The great empires of Europe stand at the height of their power — and at the brink of catastrophe. Ancient rivalries fester, colonial ambitions collide, and arms manufacturers grow fat on the paranoia of nations. Each faction pursues its own vision for Europe's future. Some seek peace; most do not. War is not inevitable — but it is very, very likely.

Players represent the leaders, generals, diplomats, and industrialists who will decide the fate of millions. The game is played over 6 turns, each representing roughly one year (1910–1915). Alliances will form, betrayals will occur, and the Game Master will ensure that history — or something like it — unfolds.

👥 Player Count & Setup

12-24 players divided into 8 factions plus 1 Game Master. Each faction has 1–5 players who collectively make decisions. Configure your player count in the Setup tab. The game is designed for two boats — factions on separate boats must communicate via written dispatches (paper notes ferried between boats), adding delicious delays and the possibility of intercepted messages.

Two-Boat Setup: Place the Central Powers (Germany, Austria-Hungary, Ottoman Empire) and one arms dealer on Boat A. Place the Entente (France, Britain, Russia) and the other arms dealer on Boat B. This forces diplomacy across the water — messengers must physically carry notes between boats. The GM floats between both.
⏱ Time & Pacing

Total game time: 2.5–3 hours.

Turns 1–3 (Peacetime): ~20 min each
Diplomacy-heavy. Build armies, forge alliances, scheme.
Turns 4–6 (Crisis/War): ~25 min each
Faster. War erupts. Battles resolve. Empires crumble.

Each turn has 4 phases: News → Diplomacy → Orders → Resolution. The GM keeps a timer and announces phase changes. Between turns, there is a 5-minute free diplomacy break.

🏛 The Factions at a Glance

+1 Game Master — controls the news, resolves disputes, and plays minor nations (Italy, Serbia, Belgium) as needed.

⚡ Arms dealers are the chaos agents of this game. They fund coups, plant fake crises, run bidding wars for superweapons, lock nations into exclusive contracts, and earn a War Dividend every turn conflict is active. They NEED war to happen — and they have the tools to make it happen.

⚠ The Inner Game — Personal Secret Objectives:
Every player has a named historical role with their OWN personal secret objectives — often in tension with their teammates. The Chancellor wants to topple the Kaiser. The French General wants reckless offensives the President opposes. The Sultan and the Young Turk are fighting for control of the Ottoman Empire. One player inside Russia is a secret revolutionary — outwardly loyal, secretly sabotaging the war effort and trying to trigger revolution. The Schneider-Škoda arms dealer is a solo "Merchant of Death" who roams between both boats, funding coups and starting wars for profit. Final victory goes to the INDIVIDUAL player with the most VP — not the faction.

You are the All-Highest. Brilliant, erratic, and obsessed with matching your cousin King George's navy. You have final say on all German decisions — unless both the Chancellor AND Chief of Staff vote to overrule you (a "Constitutional Crisis").

🏛 THE CHANCELLOR — The Pragmatist

🗝 PERSONAL SECRET:
◈ Topple the Kaiser: Engineer a "Constitutional Crisis" — get the Chief of Staff to side with you against the Kaiser on a major decision. +4 personal VP for the first overrule, +2 for a second.
◈ Diplomatic Mastermind: Personally negotiate a treaty with ANY Entente power that lasts 2+ turns. +3 personal VP.
◈ Delay the War: If Germany enters Turn 4 without being at war, +2 personal VP.
⚔ CHIEF OF GENERAL STAFF — The War Planner
Germany's top military mind. You control troop deployments and battle strategy. You have spent your career perfecting the Schlieffen Plan. You believe war is inevitable and Germany must strike first.
🗝 PERSONAL SECRET:
◈ My Legacy: If the Schlieffen Plan is executed (invasion through Belgium), +3 personal VP regardless of outcome. +3 more if it succeeds. You WANT war.
◈ Militarism: Germany must have 12+ army divisions by Turn 4. +2 personal VP. Every Gold the Kaiser wastes on ships is a soldier you don't have.
◈ The Kingmaker: The Chancellor needs your vote to overrule the Kaiser. Side with the Chancellor: +2 personal VP if it leads to a better military outcome. Stay loyal to the Kaiser: +1 personal VP (safe but boring).

🗝 PERSONAL SECRET:

◈ Avoid Revolution: Spend at least 3 Gold/turn on domestic stability. If you skip 2 consecutive turns, revolution triggers — lose half your army, –6 personal VP. This is YOUR personal nightmare.
◈ The Mystic: Once per game, consult "the mystic" (ask the GM to flip a coin). Heads: gain 3 Gold from "divine providence." Tails: lose 2 Gold to "charitable donations." You believe in this deeply.

⚔ THE WAR MINISTER — The Pan-Slavist
You command the largest army in Europe — on paper. In reality, it is poorly equipped, slowly mobilizing, and spread across a vast empire. But you believe in Russia's destiny: to unite all Slavic peoples under Moscow's protection and seize Constantinople from the Turks. The Tsar is weak. You deal with the military; the Duma Representative handles the domestic situation — or so they claim.
🗝 PERSONAL SECRET:
◈ Dominate the Balkans: Have the most influence over Serbia, Bulgaria, Romania (spend Gold to bribe, or commit troops). +4 personal VP. This is Russia's backyard.
◈ Secure French Loans: Get France to loan Russia at least 5 Gold over the game. +2 personal VP. You need their money to modernize the army.
◈ Mobilize Early: If you can convince the Tsar to begin mobilization BEFORE war is declared (pre-position troops), +3 personal VP. If the Tsar refuses, you may go behind his back by submitting secret orders to the GM — but if the Tsar finds out, –3 personal VP.
◈ Suspicious of the Duma: The Duma Representative talks of "reform" and "the people." You don't trust them. If revolution triggers and you can prove the Duma Representative contributed to it, the War Minister may declare martial law — you take sole command of the remaining army. +2 personal VP.
☭ THE DUMA REPRESENTATIVE — Voice of the People
You sit in the Russian Duma — the toothless parliament the Tsar grudgingly tolerates. You speak for reform, for the peasants, for a constitutional monarchy. The Tsar and the War Minister think you are a moderate liberal. They are wrong.
🗝 DEEP SECRET — THE REVOLUTIONARY:
You are not a liberal reformer. You are a revolutionary agent — a Bolshevik, a Socialist Revolutionary, an anarchist, pick your flavor. Your true goal is the destruction of the Tsarist state. You want Russia to LOSE the war, or better yet, to collapse into revolution so that you can seize power from the ashes.

YOUR TRUE OBJECTIVES (worth far more than faction VP):

◈ Trigger the Revolution (+8 personal VP): If revolution triggers in Russia (Tsar fails to pay stability 2 turns in a row, OR Russia loses a major battle while stability is low), you WIN BIG. You may declare yourself leader of the new Revolutionary Government. You take control of whatever remains of Russia's army. The Tsar and War Minister lose –6 VP each. This is your endgame.

◈ Sabotage the War Effort (+3 personal VP): Each turn, you may secretly submit a "sabotage order" to the GM. You pick one: (a) Divert 1 Gold from the treasury to "the people" (you pocket it — the GM quietly reduces Russia's Gold by 1), (b) Reduce one army's effectiveness (GM secretly applies –1 to Russia's next combat roll), or (c) Delay mobilization (add 1 extra turn to Russia's mobilization time). The Tsar doesn't know why things keep going wrong. +1 personal VP per successful sabotage.

◈ Agitate the Workers (+2 personal VP per success): During the Diplomacy phase, you may spend 1 Gold to "organize a strike" — the GM rolls d6. On 4+, Russia loses 1 Gold of income that turn (factory strikes). On 1-3, nothing happens. Either way, the Tsar doesn't know you caused it unless he investigates.

◈ Build the Network (+3 personal VP): Make secret contact with arms dealers. If an arms dealer uses FUND A COUP targeting Russia, and YOU are the junior player who receives the anonymous bribe — accept it. You and the arms dealer are now allies. If you can get 2+ arms dealers secretly funding your revolution, +3 personal VP.

◈ The Long Game: You still earn shared faction VP if Russia achieves its public objectives. So you're incentivized to HELP Russia early (build trust, gain access to decisions) and BETRAY Russia late (when revolution is most damaging). The Tsar may investigate you — act loyal. Attend meetings. Offer good military advice. Then, when the moment is right, burn it all down.

⚠ IF YOU ARE DISCOVERED: If the Tsar uses the Okhrana to investigate you and learns one of your secrets, you may be EXILED from the faction. If exiled, you become an independent revolutionary — you keep your personal VP and any Gold you've stolen, but lose access to Russia's army. You may then openly ally with any faction, offer intelligence about Russian troop positions, or simply watch the empire burn. Exile is survivable — discovery is just the beginning of a new game.

The Tsar and War Minister should receive their cards together — the Duma Representative receives theirs separately, with instructions to read it alone. They should then join the Russian team acting as a loyal member. The GM should keep a close eye on this player's secret orders each turn. This role is for your best actor.

◈ Every turn a MAJOR WAR is active, earn +2 bonus Gold. No war = –5 VP. You need war.
⚡ ARMS DEALER SPECIAL POWERS (Schneider-Škoda)

All the same powers as Krupp-Vickers (Fund a Coup, Plant a Crisis, Exclusive Contract, Industrial Espionage) — PLUS these solo-player advantages:

🏰 FORTIFICATION RACKET (3 Gold to buyer): Your specialty. Sell forts to BOTH sides. Nation A buys a fort, then sell Nation B a fort. Then sell Nation B the gun that cracks Nation A's fort. Each fort sale earns 3 Gold.

💣 THE SUPER-HEAVY SIEGE HOWITZER: Only ONE exists. You auction it. Starting price 6 Gold. +4 to ONE attack vs a fortified position. Run the auction personally — pit nations against each other. The markup is pure profit.

🚶 LONE WOLF — FREE MOVEMENT: Because you are solo, you may physically move between BOTH BOATS at any time during the Diplomacy phase. You are the only non-GM player with this privilege. Krupp-Vickers is split between boats; you move freely. Use this to carry information, spread lies, and play both sides.

🤝 THE DOUBLE BOOK: You may maintain two separate price lists — one for each boat. Sell armies to Germany for 2 Gold, tell France you're charging Germany 3 Gold. Nobody can verify unless they talk to each other (which requires dispatches between boats that you can intercept or delay). The GM tracks your real sales.

🔥 REGIME CHANGE SPECIALIST: Fund a Coup costs you only 2 Gold (not 3). You're more efficient at destabilization because you answer to nobody. Each successful coup (junior player acts against their leader) earns +2 VP on top of everything else.
💀 THE MERCHANT OF DEATH — One Player to Rule Them All
You are the most dangerous person at this party. You have factories in France (Schneider) and Austria-Hungary (Škoda), which means you have access to both alliance blocs' secrets. You sit on neither boat permanently — you ROAM. You sell to everyone, lie to everyone, and fund chaos everywhere. Krupp-Vickers has two people who must agree; you make every decision alone, instantly.
🗝 YOUR AGENDA:
◈ War Architect (+6 VP): If you can prove to the GM that you directly contributed to war breaking out — by funding a coup that triggered a declaration, planting a crisis that escalated, selling weapons that enabled an invasion, or bribing a hawk into action — gain +6 VP. You don't just profit from war; you CAUSE it.

◈ Puppet Master (+4 VP): Have active exclusive contracts with nations on BOTH sides of a conflict simultaneously. You are arming both armies with your weapons. +4 VP.

◈ Bankroll the Revolution (+4 VP): If you discover (or suspect) that Russia's Duma Representative is a revolutionary, secretly fund them. Send Gold to the GM marked "for the Duma Representative." If revolution triggers in Russia and you contributed financially, +4 VP. Regime change is your product.

◈ Destroy Krupp-Vickers (+3 VP): Get at least one Krupp-Vickers director nationalized, bankrupted, or cut off from their home nation's contracts. Expose their secret enemy sales to the relevant government. Undercut their prices. Steal their clients. +3 VP per director you destroy.

◈ The Insurance Policy (+2 VP): If EITHER France or Austria-Hungary collapses, you lose a factory. But if you've earned 25+ Gold by that point, declare "corporate restructuring" — you keep all VP and remaining Gold regardless. Survive the empires you helped destroy.

◈ THE ULTIMATE WIN: If you end the game with the most total VP of ANY player (beating all nations' leaders, generals, and Krupp-Vickers), you are the true winner — the one who profited while empires burned. The Merchant of Death.
GIVE THIS ROLE TO YOUR MOST SOCIALLY CHARISMATIC, DEVIOUS PLAYER. They will be running between boats, whispering in ears, making deals, and lying to everyone. They need to be comfortable talking to every group and keeping a poker face. This is the star role of the game.
📜 Core Mechanics

GOLD (💰)

Gold is the currency of power. You earn it each turn from your income, and spend it on armies, navies, diplomacy, bribes, and arms purchases. All transactions go through the GM. You may loan, gift, or bribe Gold to any other faction freely — just tell the GM.

Spending Gold:
◆ Buy 1 Army Division: 3 Gold (or 2 Gold via arms dealer)
◆ Buy 1 Fleet: 4 Gold (or 3 Gold via arms dealer)
◆ Buy Artillery Upgrade: 2 Gold via arms dealer (+1 to one attack)
◆ Fortify a region: 4 Gold direct (or 3 Gold from Schneider-Skoda, +2 defense)
◆ Diplomatic action (bribe minor nation, etc.): 2–5 Gold
◆ Espionage (learn another faction's troop positions or secret): 3 Gold → GM rolls d6, 4+ succeeds
◆ Domestic stability (Russia, Austria-Hungary special): 2–3 Gold/turn
Arms Dealer Special Actions (dealers only):
◆ Fund a Coup: 3 Gold → secretly bribe a junior player inside any nation to betray their leader
◆ Plant a Crisis: 3 Gold → write a fake news headline, GM reads it next turn as real
◆ Exclusive Contract: 2 Gold → lock a nation into buying only from you (3 turns, 1 Gold/turn fee)
◆ Industrial Espionage: 2 Gold → learn rival consortium's sales, treasury, and latest client
◆ War Dividend: passive +2 Gold/turn while any major war is active. No war = –5 VP.

ARMIES & NAVIES (⚔ / ⚓)

Army Divisions represent ~100,000 troops. Fleets represent squadrons of warships. They are used to attack regions, defend territory, blockade ports, and project power.

Combat Resolution: When armies clash, each side totals their divisions. The GM rolls 1d6 for each side. Higher roll + more divisions wins. Ties go to the defender. Losing side loses divisions equal to half the difference (rounded up, minimum 1). Winner loses 1 division. Fortifications give the defender +2 to their roll.

Naval Combat: Same as above but with fleets. Naval superiority allows blockades — deploy fleets to a sea zone and declare a blockade to cut an enemy's trade income. If the blockade persists, their industrial output suffers too. Break a blockade by winning a naval battle in that zone. Fleets also enable troop transport across water (must control sea zone to move armies across the Channel, Mediterranean, etc.).

THE MAP (Simplified Regions)

The game uses an abstract map. The GM tracks troop positions. Key regions:

Western Europe:
◆ France (French home)
◆ Paris (French capital — lose this, lose the war)
◆ Alsace-Lorraine (German-held, French want it)
◆ Belgium (neutral — invading triggers British entry)
◆ Britain (island — safe unless naval invasion)
Central/Eastern Europe:
◆ Germany (German home)
◆ Austria (Austrian home)
◆ Serbia (flashpoint — Austrian target, Russian protectorate)
◆ Russia (vast — can absorb invasion but slow to mobilize)
◆ Poland (contested borderland)
Southern/Mediterranean:
◆ Ottoman Empire (controls the Straits)
◆ The Dardanelles (strategic chokepoint)
◆ Italy (neutral — can be swayed by either side)
◆ The Balkans (powder keg)
Seas:
◆ North Sea (British–German naval rivalry)
◆ English Channel (must control to invade Britain)
◆ Mediterranean (Ottoman/French/British interests)
◆ Baltic Sea (Russian/German access)

DIPLOMACY (🤝)

The heart of the game. During the Diplomacy phase, players can:

◆ Form Alliances: Written, signed agreements (on paper!) between factions. Alliances are only binding if both parties present the signed document to the GM. Breaking an alliance costs –2 VP but is entirely legal.

◆ Trade: Exchange Gold, promise future payments, or trade arms.

◆ Threaten: Promise consequences if a faction takes a certain action.

◆ Send Dispatches: Between boats, messages must be written on paper and physically carried. The GM may "intercept" a dispatch (roll 1d6: on a 1, the message is read aloud to all).

◆ Secret Deals: Any deal not shown to the GM is not enforceable but IS possible. Gentleman's agreements, backroom deals, betrayals — all fair game.

DECLARING WAR (🔥)

Any nation may declare war on any other during the Orders phase. When war is declared:

◆ All existing alliances are tested — allied nations must declare if they honor their alliance within the same turn or break it.

◆ Russia requires 1 full turn to mobilize (can only use half their army divisions in the first turn of war).

◆ Britain automatically enters the war if Belgium is invaded.

◆ Arms dealers may continue selling to all sides unless nationalized by their home government.

🏆 Victory Conditions

At the end of Turn 6, each player tallies their Victory Points. There are TWO types of VP:

Faction VP — earned from shared faction objectives. Every player in the faction receives these equally.

Personal VP — earned from your individual role card's secret objectives. ONLY YOU receive these. Your teammates don't know what you're after — and their goals may conflict with yours.

Final score = Faction VP + Personal VP. The INDIVIDUAL player with the highest combined total wins the game. Not the faction — the person. This means teammates are also competitors. The German Chancellor might sabotage the Kaiser to score personal VP, even if it hurts Germany slightly. The French General might launch a reckless offensive the President opposes. The Sultan and Young Turk might be tearing each other apart while Russia invades.

Awards at Game End:
◆ Grand Victor: Player with the most total VP
◆ Best Faction: Faction with the highest combined total across all members
◆ Merchant of Death: Arms dealer player with the most personal VP
◆ Best Betrayal: GM's award for the most dramatic backstab (faction VP or personal)
◆ The Peacemaker: GM's award for the player who tried hardest to prevent war (whether they succeeded or not)

🎩 Game Master's Guide

You are the most important person in this game. You are the referee, the narrator, the minor nations, and the voice of history. Here is everything you need to run a smooth, dramatic, 2.5-hour experience.

BEFORE THE GAME (30 min setup)

◆ Print faction sheets (from the Factions tab) — one per team. Fold secret objectives so they're hidden until the team opens them.

◆ Prepare Gold tokens — use poker chips, coins, candy, or numbered slips of paper. Each faction starts with their listed treasury.

◆ Set up a simple map — draw the regions on a large piece of paper or whiteboard. Use colored tokens (coins, game pieces, bottle caps) for armies and fleets.

◆ Assign factions. Recommended: put your most theatrical players as Germany and Britain (they have the most to do). Put cunning players as arms dealers. Put competitive players as France and Russia.

◆ Assign boats. Central Powers + Krupp-Vickers on Boat A. Entente + Schneider-Skoda on Boat B.

◆ Get dice (at least 2d6), a timer (phone works), and paper for dispatches.

RUNNING EACH TURN

Phase 1 — NEWS (3 min): Read the headline for the current turn dramatically. Explain the game effect. Answer questions. Let it sink in.

Phase 2 — DIPLOMACY (8–10 min): Set a timer. Players talk, scheme, bribe, threaten. Move between boats to deliver dispatches. You should circulate and eavesdrop. Remind factions of their objectives if they seem lost. Stir the pot.

Phase 3 — ORDERS (3 min): Each faction writes their orders on paper and hands them to you simultaneously. Orders include: troop movements, purchases, declarations of war, alliance announcements, and any Gold transactions.

Phase 4 — RESOLUTION (5 min): Read all orders aloud. Resolve purchases first, then movements, then combat. Collect and distribute Gold. Announce results dramatically.

If the game is going slowly, skip to the Assassination event early (Turn 3 instead of 4). If factions are too peaceful, have Serbia provoke Austria, or have an arms dealer leak intelligence. Your job is to create drama.

PLAYING MINOR NATIONS

You control Italy, Serbia, Belgium, Bulgaria, Romania, and other minor nations. Use them to:

◆ Serbia: Fiercely nationalist. Will resist Austria. May accept Russian protection. If Austria attacks without overwhelming force, Serbia fights back with 3 divisions.

◆ Italy: Opportunistic. Will join whichever side offers the most territory. Default price: 8 Gold + promise of Austrian territory. Can be swayed by either bloc.

◆ Belgium: Neutral. Will resist invasion with 2 divisions. If invaded, Britain MUST respond (per their objectives).

◆ Bulgaria/Romania: Available for bidding. Whoever pays more Gold gets 2 extra divisions on their side.

PACING TRICKS

◆ If Turn 3 ends with no war declared, read a BONUS crisis event: "Archduke Franz Ferdinand has been assassinated in Sarajevo." Austria MUST respond — either declare war on Serbia or suffer –3 VP and internal collapse.

◆ If one side is dominating, introduce a neutral intervention: Italy joins the underdog, or the USA offers loans (5 Gold) to the weaker alliance.

◆ In the final turn, announce that all secret objectives will be revealed — this creates a frenzy of last-minute betrayals and gambits.

◆ End the game with a dramatic reading of the final state of Europe. Who won? Who lost? Who profited from the carnage? Award the VP, crown the victor, and raise a glass to the fallen empires.

The Golden Rule of GMing: Your job is not to be fair. Your job is to make the game dramatic, memorable, and fun. If a rule doesn't serve the story, bend it. If a player has a brilliant idea not covered by the rules, say yes and roll a die.
