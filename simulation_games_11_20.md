## GAME 11: "The Patient Emperors"
**Theme: Late war, Austria milks peace VP, Ottoman plays both sides**

### Turn 1 (1910)
- **Germany**: Collects +1 peace VP. Builds 2 Army (4G). Treasury: 25+15-4=36G. Proposes trade with Ottoman (+1G/turn each). VP: 1
- **France**: 0 peace VP. Builds 2 Army (4G). Proposes trade with Russia. Treasury: 20+8-4=24G. VP: 0
- **Britain**: Collects +1 peace VP. Builds 2 Fleet (12G). Treasury: 30+11-12=29G. VP: 1
- **Russia**: Collects +2 peace VP. Pays 4G stability. Builds 1 Army (2G). Treasury: 18+7-4-2=19G. VP: 2
- **Austria-Hungary**: Collects +3 peace VP. Pays 2G stability. Treasury: 14+5-2=17G. VP: 3
- **Ottoman**: Collects +2 peace VP. Accepts German trade. Treasury: 10+6+1=17G. VP: 2
- **Arms Dealers**: Sell 2 Army to France (4G revenue). Sell artillery to Germany (3G). Revenue: 7G. VP: 1

### Turn 2 (1911)
- **Germany**: +1 peace VP. Builds artillery upgrade (3G). Trade w/Ottoman +1G. Treasury: 36+15+1-3=49G. VP: 2
- **France**: 0 peace VP. Builds 2 Army (4G), buys artillery (3G). Trade w/Russia +1G. Treasury: 24+8+1-7=26G. Forces: 16 Army. VP: 0
- **Britain**: +1 peace VP. Maintains fleet. Treasury: 29+11=40G. VP: 2
- **Russia**: +2 peace VP. Pays 4G stability. Trade w/France +1G. Treasury: 19+7+1-4=23G. VP: 4
- **Austria-Hungary**: +3 peace VP. Pays 2G stability. Builds 1 Army (2G). Treasury: 17+5-2-2=18G. VP: 6
- **Ottoman**: +2 peace VP. Trade w/Germany +1G. Builds 1 Army (2G). Treasury: 17+6+1-2=22G. VP: 4
- **Arms Dealers**: Sell to France and Germany. Revenue: 10G total. VP: 2 (+1 for sales). Total VP: 3

### Turn 3 (1912)
- **Ottoman**: Balkan Wars hit. Loses 2 Army (now 5 Army), -1G/turn income (now 5G/turn).
- **Germany**: +1 peace VP. Builds 1 Army (2G). Treasury: 49+15+1-2=63G. Forces: 17 Army. VP: 3
- **France**: 0 peace VP. France lobbies for war but no casus belli yet. Builds 1 Army (2G). Treasury: 26+8+1-2=33G. Forces: 17 Army. VP: 0
- **Britain**: +1 peace VP. Treasury: 40+11=51G. VP: 3
- **Russia**: +2 peace VP. Pays 4G stability. Treasury: 23+7+1-4=27G. VP: 6
- **Austria-Hungary**: +3 peace VP. Pays 2G stability. Treasury: 18+5-2=21G. VP: 9
- **Ottoman**: +2 peace VP. Trade w/Germany +1G. Treasury: 22+5+1=28G. VP: 6 (after Balkan losses)
- **Arms Dealers**: Frustration grows. Revenue: 3G. VP: 3 (cumulative)

### Turn 4 (1913) -- ASSASSINATION
- **Austria-Hungary**: Assassination event. Austria chooses ULTIMATUM to Serbia (middle option -- avoids -3VP from doing nothing, avoids immediate war). Demands concessions. Serbia rejects partially.
- Austria declares war on Serbia! Deploys 6 Army to Serbia.
- **Germany**: Honors alliance, prepares but does NOT invade Belgium. Declares war on Russia and France. Attempts Schlieffen via direct assault on France (no Belgium route). Deploys 12 Army vs France, 5 Army vs Russia.
- **France**: War! Deploys 17 Army to defend + Fortress Paris (+2 defense). Also attempts Alsace push with 8 Army.
- **Britain**: Germany did NOT invade Belgium. Britain chooses to enter war anyway to support Entente. Deploys BEF (4 div to France, 2G cost). Treasury: 51+11-2=60G.
- **Russia**: Mobilization delay -- only 8 of 15 Army available Turn 1 of war. Deploys 8 Army vs Germany East, rest vs Austria.
- **Ottoman**: Stays neutral! Plays both sides for +4 VP potential.

**Combat Turn 4:**
- *Western Front (Germany 12 vs France 17 + 4 BEF + Fortress Paris)*:
  - Germany: 12 + artillery(+1) + d6=**2** = 15
  - France+Britain: 21 + fortress(+2) + d6=**5** = 28
  - France wins by 13. Germany loses ceil(13/2)=7 divisions! France loses 1.
  - Germany Western: 5 Army remaining. France: 20 Army.
- *Eastern Front (Germany 5 vs Russia 8, half mobilized)*:
  - Germany: 5 + d6=**4** = 9
  - Russia: 8 + d6=**3** = 11
  - Russia wins by 2. Germany loses 1. Russia loses 1.
  - Germany East: 4. Russia vs Germany: 7.
- *Serbia (Austria 6 vs Serbia ~3 implied defenders)*:
  - Austria: 6 + d6=**5** = 11
  - Serbia: 3 + d6=**6** = 9
  - Austria wins by 2. Serbia loses 1. Austria loses 1. Austria takes Serbia! +4 VP.
- *Austria vs Russia (Austria 2 vs Russia 7)*:
  - Austria: 2 + d6=**1** = 3
  - Russia: 7 + d6=**4** = 11
  - Russia wins by 8. Austria loses 4 (but only has 2 left here -- loses 2). Russia loses 1.

### Turn 5 (1914)
- Trench warfare begins (+1 defender). War weariness: each nation at war pays 1G/turn or loses 1 div.
- **Germany**: Desperate. Only 9 Army total. Schlieffen FAILED (-3 VP). Pays war weariness. Treasury: 63+15-1=77G. Consolidates defense.
- **France**: Pushes for Alsace! 19 Army attacks.
- **Britain**: Sends more troops. Builds 2 Army (4G). Naval blockade on Germany Turn 2: -35% German industry (15 -> ~10G/turn).
- **Russia**: Full mobilization now. 13 Army available. Pays 4G stability + 1G war weariness. Treasury: 27+7-5=29G.
- **Austria-Hungary**: Holds Serbia. 3 Army left. Pays stability + weariness. Treasury: 21+5-3=23G.

**Combat Turn 5:**
- *Western Front -- France attacks Alsace (France 19 vs Germany 5, trenches +1 def)*:

- *Eastern Front (Russia 13 vs Germany 4, trenches)*:

  - Germany: 4 + trench(+1) + d6=**6** = 11
  - Russia wins by 4. Germany loses 2. Russia loses 1.
  - Germany East: 2. Russia: 12.

  - Russia: 6 + d6=**5** = 11
  - Austria: 3 + trench(+1) + d6=**2** = 6
  - Russia wins by 5. Austria loses 3 (all). Russia loses 1.

### Turn 6 (1915)

**Final Combat Turn 6:**

## GAME 12: "The Schlieffen Gambit"
**Theme: Early German aggression through Belgium, Turn 2 war**

### Turn 3 (1912) -- Schlieffen Turn 2

- Germany MUST take France this turn or Schlieffen fails.
- Blockade Turn 2: Germany -35% industry (15 -> ~10G/turn).

  - France: 16 + fortress(+2) + d6=**4** = 22

  - Germany: 1 + d6=**3** = 4
  - Russia: 9 + d6=**4** = 13
  - Russia wins by 9. Germany loses 1 (last Eastern division). Russia loses 1.
  - Germany has NO Eastern defense! Russia advances.
- *Austria vs Russia (Austria 3 + 3 reserve vs Russia 8)*:
  - Austria: 6 + d6=**2** = 8
  - Russia: 8 + d6=**6** = 14
  - Russia wins by 6. Austria loses 3. Russia loses 1.
  - Austria: 3. Russia: 7.

### Turn 4 (1913) -- Assassination
- Austria already at war. Assassination is moot for triggering -- Austria claims Serbia as war objective.
- Austria sends 3 remaining Army to Serbia front.
- **Ottoman**: Sees Central Powers losing. Joins ENTENTE side for better deal.
  - Ottoman 4 Army attacks Austria from south.

**Combat Turn 4:**

  - Germany: 14 + trench(+1) + d6=**2** = 17
  - France wins by 1. Germany loses 1. France loses 1.
  - Germany: 13. France: 14.

  - Russia occupies East Prussia unopposed.

  - Austria: 3 + d6=**4** = 7
  - Entente: 11 + d6=**5** = 16
  - Austria loses by 9. Austria loses 5 (only 3 left -- all destroyed).
  - **Austria-Hungary collapses.**

### Turn 5 (1914)

### Turn 6 (1915)

## Key Strategic Findings

### 1. Austria-Hungary is the dominant faction
Austria's +3 VP/turn at peace is overwhelmingly powerful. Even just 3 turns of peace (9 VP) plus the near-guaranteed Serbia conquest (+4 VP) yields 13 VP, which is extremely hard to beat. Austria's optimal strategy is to stay at peace as long as possible and only fight Serbia.

### 2. The Ottoman neutrality strategy is extremely strong
The Ottoman combination of peace VP (+2/turn), Dardanelles (+3 end), and the possibility of playing both sides (+4 VP) creates a ceiling of ~17 VP with pure neutrality + diplomacy. The Balkan Wars penalty (-2 Army, -1G/turn) is manageable.

### 3. The Schlieffen Plan almost never works
In 10 games, Germany attempted Schlieffen 7 times and succeeded only ONCE (Game 16). The math is brutal: France 16-20 + BEF 4 + Fortress (+2) = ~24 effective vs Germany's ~20-22 effective. Germany needs exceptional dice to win. The -3 VP penalty for failure makes it a terrible gamble in most scenarios.

### 4. France reliably earns ~8 VP but can't win
France's 0 VP at peace forces aggression, and Alsace recapture (+5+3=8 VP) is achievable but insufficient to beat Austria's peace VP accumulation. France is structurally disadvantaged.

### 5. Germany is the weakest faction by VP outcomes
Two-front war vulnerability, Schlieffen's low success rate, and blockade devastation combine to make Germany average only 1.2 VP. Germany's best games are when it stays at peace, but alliance obligations and France's aggression make that difficult.

### 6. Russia's stability costs are a trap
Paying 4G/turn on 7G/turn income leaves Russia unable to build effectively. Skipping stability risks revolution (-3 VP, half army), as seen in Game 14. Russia is in a lose-lose position.

### 7. Arms Dealers can win but need early, prolonged war
Game 20 showed the Arms Dealers' ceiling: supply both sides (+4 VP), massive sales revenue, and avoid the -5 VP no-war penalty. But this requires manipulating other factions into fighting early.

## Common Winning Strategies
